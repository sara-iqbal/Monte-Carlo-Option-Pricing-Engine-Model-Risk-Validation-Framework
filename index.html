Assumption Violations
Model risk implication of each failure
✗
Fat Tails — OTM Options Underpriced
Real returns have excess kurtosis of +1.51. GBM underestimates extreme moves. Model systematically underprices deep OTM puts and calls — requires reserve uplift for tail positions.
✗
Volatility Clustering — Stochastic Vol Needed
Squared returns show autocorrelation (ρ²=0.18, p<0.001). Volatility is not constant — it clusters. Heston or SABR stochastic vol models are more appropriate for tail risk management.
✗
Volatility Smile — Flat Vol Assumption Fails
Market prices OTM puts at higher implied vol than ATM (put skew). Flat vol model misprices these options by up to $0.87 per contract. Local vol or SABR required for skew-sensitive positions.
✓
Return Independence — GBM Assumption Holds
Lag-1 autocorrelation of −0.002 (p=0.83) confirms returns are serially uncorrelated. GBM's independence assumption is not violated on daily data.

Reserve Framework — Four Components
1. Model Spread
$0.0015
Difference between the highest and lowest price across all three independent models. Represents pure model disagreement.
2. Statistical Uncertainty
$0.0200
2 × MC standard error. Captures the simulation noise in the Monte Carlo estimate. Reduces as path count increases.
3. Parameter Sensitivity
$0.1880
Price change when vol is bumped by 1 vol point. Captures the reserve needed for vol parameter uncertainty.
4. Assumption Violation
$0.1747
Cornish-Fisher fat tail correction for excess kurtosis. Captures the reserve for GBM's normality assumption being violated.
